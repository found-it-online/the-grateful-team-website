## 1. WAM Event Details page copy

### Hero

**Headline:** Three days. Three hundred miles. One pretty good reason.

**Intro:** The Wish-A-Mile Bicycle Tour is Make-A-Wish Michigan's biggest fundraiser of the year — a fully supported ride across the state that turns a long weekend on a bike into wishes granted for kids fighting critical illnesses.

---

### The Event

WAM (we just call it WAM) is a three-day, fully supported tour put on by Make-A-Wish Michigan. It started in 1987 with four friends who decided 300 miles sounded like a reasonable challenge — and it grew into a "moving city" of riders, volunteers, support crews, and Wish kids who line the route to cheer you in. [^1] [^2]

There are three ways to ride: 100, 200, or 300 miles. All of them start and finish at Marshall High School in Marshall, MI. The 300 is the full enchilada — three days, two overnights, and the kind of finish line you don't forget. [^2] [^3]

---

### Dates

**Friday, July 24 – Sunday, July 26, 2026.** The 39th annual ride. Mark it now; we leave Grand Rapids together. [^1]

---

### Location & Route

WAM starts and finishes at Marshall High School in Marshall, Michigan. From there, the 300-mile route loops through southwest Michigan — picture quiet country roads, lake breezes, and small towns that turn out to wave you through. [^2] [^3]

**Route summary (300-mile):**

| Day | From → To | Approx. Miles |
|-----|-----------|---------------|
| Friday | Marshall → Holland | ~100 [^4] |
| Saturday | Holland → Kalamazoo | ~100 [^4] |
| Sunday | Kalamazoo → Marshall | ~100 [^4] |

Riders who want a shorter taste can do the 100-mile day (Saturday, Holland → Kalamazoo) or the 200-mile two-day option. [^5] `[CONFIRM with Luis: exact 200-mile breakdown for 2026]`

---

### What to Expect

- **Fully supported.** SAG vehicles, medics, mechanics, and police escorts on the route. You ride; they handle the rest. [^2]
- **Rest stops every ~10–15 miles.** Snacks, water, sunscreen, friendly faces. `[CONFIRM stop spacing with Luis or the 2026 rider guide]`
- **Catered meals.** Breakfast, lunch, dinner — included. You will not go hungry. [^2]
- **Overnight stops.** Two nights at host high schools (Holland and Kalamazoo). Most riders camp on-site or grab a nearby hotel. Shuttles run between the two. [^5]
- **The finish line.** Wish kids and families lining the road into Marshall. This is the part nobody talks about ahead of time, because there's no way to oversell it. You'll see.

---

### Fundraising Commitment

Every rider raises a minimum of **$1,200** to participate (virtual riders raise $1,000 by July 1). [^6] That money goes directly to Make-A-Wish Michigan, which uses it to grant wishes for Michigan kids with critical illnesses. The team helps you hit your number — we've done this before, and we'll set you up with templates, tips, and donor-friendly nudges that actually work.

## 3. FAQ page copy

### 1. Do I need to be in elite shape?

Nope. We've got 60-year-olds and 25-year-olds, weekend warriors and people training for their first century. WAM is a challenge, but it's not a race — and the team rides at every pace. If you can commit to training through the spring, we'll get you to the start line.

### 2. What does it cost to join?

Joining the team is free. WAM itself has a registration fee through Make-A-Wish Michigan, and then there's the fundraising minimum (see next question). Your real costs are gear (we've got a page for that) and gas to training rides.

### 3. Is there a fundraising minimum?

Yes — Make-A-Wish Michigan requires every rider to raise at least **$1,200** to participate in WAM (or $1,000 if you're a virtual rider, due July 1). [^7] We help. The team has templates, donor lists from past years, and a few tricks that make hitting the minimum way less scary than it sounds.

### 4. I've never done a long ride — can I still join?

Yes. Most of us came in the same way. If you've got a bike and a few months of runway, training rides will get you ready. We've got people who'll ride with you at whatever pace you need — no one gets dropped.

### 5. What gear do I need to start?

A working bike, a helmet, and shoes you can ride in. That's it for showing up to a Saturday training ride. For WAM itself, you'll want the full kit (bibs, gloves, lights, hydration setup) — we built a whole gear page for that. Don't go buy everything on day one.

### 6. How does training work?

We run group rides through the spring and summer — pace groups for everyone, route shared in advance, no-drop policy on the slower rides. `[CONFIRM with Luis: weekly day/time/meet point]` Most veteran riders also do solo miles during the week. By June you'll be ready for centuries.

### 7. What's the time commitment?

Honestly? A few hours a week of riding (more as the season ramps up), plus the WAM weekend itself (July 24–26, 2026). Fundraising takes some evenings spread across the spring. You don't have to disappear from your life — you just have to be intentional about a few months of it.

### 8. Can my partner / friend / dog come?

Friends and partners — yes, please. Bring them to training rides, bring them to the post-ride hangs, bring them to the WAM finish line in Marshall. Dogs we love but generally leave at home for the actual rides. If your friend wants to ride, send them the join form.

### 9. Where does the money actually go?

Make-A-Wish Michigan. They use the funds to grant wishes for Michigan kids fighting critical illnesses — trips, experiences, the whole thing. WAM is their largest annual fundraiser, and 100% of what you raise flows through them, not us. [^8]

### 10. How do I get on the team for 2026?

Fill out the join form on our home page. We'll reach out, get you into the group chats, point you toward the Make-A-Wish Michigan registration page, and get you on the next training ride. Don't overthink it — just say hey.
